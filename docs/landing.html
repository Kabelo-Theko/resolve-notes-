RUN Nº 01 · THE PRESS

Closing a ticket rolls the press.

When a ticket resolves, resolve-notes drafts the print from what's already there — the thread, the diff, the metric that proved the fix — and hands it to the resolver for the stamp.

Three blocks, no essay.

A print is deliberately small: cause, fix, gotcha. Five minutes of the resolver's time while the context is still warm — not a documentation shift nobody scheduled.

The draft arrives pre-cited: the fix line links the diff, the verification links the metric, the gotcha quotes the thread. The resolver edits, then stamps. An unstamped print never enters the archive.

The stamp is the whole trust model: every print is signed by the person who actually did the fixing — not by a model, not by a manager, not by "the team".
